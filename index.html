How It Started / How It's Going

Self-taught. Battle-tested. Dad-powered.

My coding journey didn’t start in a classroom — it started when life hit rock bottom. In late 2023, I was 25, newly single, a father, and honestly... lost. But I made a choice: build a better life for my daughter — and maybe even enjoy the process.

One sleepless night I found Pirate Software on YouTube. His content lit a fire under me. I joined his Discord, picked up Python, C#, C++, and found Harvard’s free CS50. I started grinding. Building web apps, learning databases, struggling through tutorials, and eventually finished a 2D game in Godot. Took six brutal months, but I got it done.

I’ve learned I don’t need to be the best. I just need to keep going. I work fast, learn hard, and care about making things that actually work. This isn’t just about code. It’s about purpose.

No roadmap, just grit, code, and a reason to keep going.
